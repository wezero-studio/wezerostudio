### Process Section

**Section label:** How we work

**Headline:**
From brief to browser in four stages.

**Stages:**

1. **Discovery**
   We start with a conversation. What are you building, who's it for, and what does success look like? We ask until we're clear, because ambiguity at the start always costs more later.

2. **Design**
   No templates. We design from scratch, for your product and your users. Figma wireframes, refined to high-fidelity, reviewed with you at every stage.

3. **Build**
   We code it. TypeScript, Next.js, Tailwind — modern stack with no legacy baggage. We write tests, document the tricky parts, and deploy to production-ready infrastructure.

4. **Launch & Beyond**
   We ship it, we monitor it, and we stick around. Post-launch support, analytics review, and iteration — we don't disappear after the handoff.

### Our Story

**Section label:** How we started

**Headline:**
Built out of frustration with how agencies work.

**Body:**
We'd all been on the client side of bad agency relationships. Missed deadlines, bloated proposals, junior devs shipped as senior talent, and websites that looked great in the pitch deck but fell apart in production.

So we built the kind of agency we'd actually want to hire.

Wezero started as a small team with a simple rule: only take projects we can do well, and do them right. That rule hasn't changed.

### Values

**Section label:** What we believe

**Headline:**
A few things we won't compromise on.

**Values:**

1. **Craft over speed**
   We move fast, but we don't cut corners. The difference between a site that lasts two years and one that needs a full rebuild in six months is almost always craft.

2. **Honest scoping**
   We tell you what things will cost before we start, not after. If a scope changes, we talk about it.

3. **Ownership, not dependency**
   When we deliver, you own it — the repo, the assets, the docs. We want you to be able to run without us, not locked in.

4. **Small on purpose**
   We stay small because quality degrades with scale. We'd rather build fewer things and build them better.
